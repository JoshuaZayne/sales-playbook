# Sales Methodology Documentation

## Overview

This playbook implements a consultative sales methodology optimized for enterprise SaaS deals in the financial services vertical.

## Sales Process Stages

### 1. Initial Discovery
**Goal**: Understand current state and establish rapport

Key activities:
- Research the prospect before the meeting
- Ask open-ended questions about their business
- Identify high-level pain points
- Map the organizational structure
- Qualify for BANT criteria

Questions focus on:
- Current data strategy and capabilities
- Competitive pressures from digital banks
- Strategic priorities for the year
- Initial stakeholder mapping

### 2. Technical Discovery
**Goal**: Deep dive on technical requirements and integration

Key activities:
- Map the technical architecture
- Identify integration requirements
- Assess IT bandwidth and constraints
- Address security and compliance concerns
- Involve technical stakeholders

Questions focus on:
- Current tech stack and data architecture
- API capabilities and preferences
- Security certifications required
- Deployment preferences

### 3. Business Case Development
**Goal**: Quantify the value and build ROI

Key activities:
- Calculate current state costs
- Estimate efficiency gains
- Build custom ROI model
- Identify success metrics
- Define business outcomes

Questions focus on:
- Current costs and inefficiencies
- Member/customer lifetime value
- Attrition rates and drivers
- Budget and approval process

### 4. Decision Stage
**Goal**: Navigate the buying process to close

Key activities:
- Map the decision process
- Engage the economic buyer
- Address final objections
- Negotiate terms
- Close the deal

Questions focus on:
- Evaluation criteria and timeline
- Competitive alternatives
- Risk factors and blockers
- Next steps to close

## Qualification Frameworks

### BANT Scoring

| Criterion | Weight | Scoring |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Budget | 25% | 0-25 based on allocated budget |
| Authority | 25% | 0-25 based on decision maker level |
| Need | 25% | 0-25 based on urgency |
| Timeline | 25% | 0-25 based on implementation window |

**Grade Thresholds**:
- HOT: 80+ points
- WARM: 60-79 points
- DEVELOPING: 40-59 points
- COLD: <40 points

### MEDDIC Framework

| Criterion | Assessment |
|-----------|------------|
| Metrics | Success metrics defined? |
| Economic Buyer | Access to budget holder? |
| Decision Criteria | Evaluation criteria clear? |
| Decision Process | Approval process mapped? |
| Identify Pain | Compelling pain identified? |
| Champion | Internal advocate engaged? |

## Objection Handling

### Common Categories

1. **Price Objections**
   - Focus on total value and ROI
   - Compare to cost of inaction
   - Offer pilot programs

2. **Competitor Objections**
   - Differentiate on data quality
   - Offer side-by-side comparison
   - Provide customer references

3. **Timing Objections**
   - Understand competing priorities
   - Show alignment with initiatives
   - Offer flexible start dates

4. **Integration Objections**
   - Highlight pre-built connectors
   - Minimize IT involvement
   - Offer implementation support

5. **Security Objections**
   - Lead with certifications
   - Offer security review
   - Provide deployment options

## ROI Calculation

### Standard Formula

```
Annual Savings = Current Cost × Efficiency Gain
Net Annual Benefit = Annual Savings - Annual License
Total Investment = Implementation + Annual License
ROI % = (3-Year Net Benefit / Total Investment) × 100
Payback (months) = Implementation / Net Annual Benefit × 12
```

### Typical Benchmarks

- Average ROI: 300%+ over 3 years
- Typical payback: 6-12 months
- Efficiency gains: 25-40%

## Best Practices

1. **Always do research** before customer meetings
2. **Listen more than you talk** during discovery
3. **Quantify everything** with specific metrics
4. **Build champions** who advocate internally
5. **Address objections** proactively
6. **Follow up promptly** after every interaction
7. **Document everything** in CRM
